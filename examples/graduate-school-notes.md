# Graduate School Decision Sketch

This is an experimental manual example, not yet a first-class script.

Inputs:

```text
study years: 2
annual tuition + living cost: $30,000
direct work salary: $100,000
post-grad expected salary: $150,000
purpose: knowledge and credential
loan required: yes
savings: $10,000
risk tolerance: low
```

Key derivation:

```text
cash cost = $30,000 * 2 = $60,000
opportunity cost = $100,000 * 2 = $200,000
total opportunity cost = $260,000
salary premium = $150,000 - $100,000 = $50,000/year
payback = $260,000 / $50,000 = 5.2 years after graduation
```

Decision insight:

```text
Post-grad salary being higher only means the graduate-school path catches up faster.
It does not mean the path has already won.
```

Break-even examples:

```text
$150,000 post-grad salary: about 5.2 years to break even after graduation
$180,000 post-grad salary: about 3.25 years to break even after graduation
$186,667 post-grad salary: about 3 years to break even after graduation
```
